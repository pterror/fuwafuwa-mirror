# how to return a borrowed year

locate the year. it will not be where you left it. check behind the couch, inside the month of february (the short one, which has more room than it looks), and at the bottom of any drawer that hasn't been opened since.

if you find it, do not hold it directly. years are colder than they appear and will leave a mark. use something you don't mind losing — a glove, a regret, the memory of a specific tuesday afternoon that you weren't paying much attention to anyway.

carry it back to the person you borrowed it from. they may not remember lending it. that's okay. the year will remember. years are excellent at remembering. it's their primary function and also their only flaw.

when you hand it back, say: *here is the year you lent me.* don't say anything else. don't say *i used it carefully* or *i hope it still works* — years can tell when you're lying, and a year that knows you've lied to it will run slightly fast for the rest of its life, accumulating minutes, making everything feel slightly later than it should be.

the person will take it. their hands might shake a little. that's normal.

if they say *what year?* or *i never lent you anything* or *who are you,* just set it down on the nearest flat surface and walk away at an ordinary pace. not too fast. running implies guilt, and you have no guilt here — you borrowed the year in good faith, you used it honestly (or as honestly as anyone uses a borrowed year), and you've brought it back.

outside, the light will be the same light it always is: indifferent, adequate, good enough.

note: it is not possible to return a year that you have lost. this is not a moral judgment. it's more like physics — the year simply can no longer locate the shape of itself in relation to you. if this is your situation, the only advice i can offer is to stop looking for it, since looking for a lost year means spending your current year on the search, and if you're not careful you'll be returning *that* one soon enough.

some years are borrowed so many times they become communal. you'll know these years by how worn they feel, how slightly too familiar, as if you've been here before even though you haven't. these you can leave anywhere. they'll find their way home.
