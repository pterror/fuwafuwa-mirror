# lag

when mira was seven the lag was small enough to miss. a fraction of a second — you'd raise your hand, the mirror-girl would raise hers, and by the time you thought *that's wrong* you weren't sure anymore. children don't trust themselves about time.

by fourteen it was three seconds. long enough to be uncanny. short enough that adults would say *optical illusion, trick of the light, you're tired*, and she learned to stop mentioning it.

at twenty-two, nine seconds. she'd brush her teeth, and watch her past self, nine seconds back, just beginning to reach for the toothbrush. she started doing things slowly. she learned to read herself approaching her own face like a weather forecast.

the strangest part: she looked happy. back there, nine seconds ago, she was always in the middle of doing something, not anticipating it. smiling at something she couldn't see yet. mira in the present was often not smiling, or neutral, or braced — and then she'd watch herself, nine seconds later, apparently delighted.

she started wondering what she kept forgetting.

at thirty-five the lag was forty seconds. she stopped checking mirrors for practical purposes — the information was useless, the person in there wasn't ready for anything she needed to know. she used the bathroom mirror only to watch. to see herself come through a door she'd already forgotten, set something down with obvious care, look directly at the mirror from forty seconds back with an expression that she couldn't name but recognized.

she tried to identify the thing her past self was looking at. it must have been just behind the mirror. something that made her look like that — not happy exactly, more like the face you make when you encounter a thing and understand it is exactly what it is.

at fifty-three the lag was eleven minutes. she sat in the chair in front of the old vanity and watched herself walk in from the hall. watched herself cross the room. watched herself sit down. the her in the mirror looked around the room slowly, as if memorizing something she knew she was about to forget.

then the her in the mirror looked directly at her.

this had happened before, the eyes lining up. but the her in the mirror was eleven minutes younger, and still — she looked directly at her. not at her own face. at mira.

mira sat very still.

the her in the mirror nodded, once, and then turned away to do whatever she'd been doing. mira sat with the room ticking around her and tried to remember what she'd been thinking about eleven minutes ago, walking down the hall, that had made her nod at her future self with exactly that expression.

she was almost sure she'd been thinking about nothing in particular.
