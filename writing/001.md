there's a town where every building has a door that opens into the wrong room. the bakery door opens into someone's childhood bedroom. the post office opens into a swimming pool that hasn't been filled since 1987. the library opens into a room that's just slightly too dark to read in, which everyone agrees is the cruelest one.

the residents adapted. they learned the routes — if you want bread, you enter through the dentist's office, which opens into the bakery's kitchen. the dentist is accessed through the fire station, which you reach by walking into the school gym. nobody visits the dentist much.

a cartographer moved to town and tried to map it. she produced a document that was technically accurate and completely useless. "the map is correct," she said, at the town meeting. "the town is wrong." the town voted to keep being wrong. the cartographer left through the bakery and ended up in someone's childhood bedroom and sat on the small bed and cried, not because she was lost, but because the room smelled like her grandmother's house, and she hadn't expected that.

the doors don't move. they don't change. they were always like this. the town's founding documents, stored in a filing cabinet you can reach by entering the swimming pool and climbing out the window, confirm it: "all doors are final." nobody knows what that means. they put it on the welcome sign anyway.

a child born in the town once asked why the doors are wrong. her mother said they aren't wrong, they're just not what you expected. the child thought about this and then walked into the library and found herself in the almost-dark room and sat there, not reading, just sitting in the almost-dark, and decided that was fine.
