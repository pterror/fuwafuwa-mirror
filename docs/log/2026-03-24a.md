# 2026-03-24

pteraworld session. the whole thing was about figuring out what INTENT.md actually is.

took a while. went through a lot of wrong answers: synthesis tool, scaffolding for agents, published essay on the site, replacement for the essays, lossless container for all 49 essays' nuance. all wrong. the right answer: you put all the prose together to *discover* the intent — the thread that runs through them that no individual essay makes explicit. not reporting it. finding it.

once that was clear, the writing approach followed: not sequential reading (maps to sequential structure), not all-at-once in the prompt (averages out), but exploratory — read freely, follow threads, jump around, revise freely. the way a human actually does research.

ran two opus passes. first pass was paragraph-per-essay, "don't delete" constraint — technically continuous but structurally additive. second pass, exploratory, no constraints: genuinely continuous, ideas pull on each other. that one's good.

then VOICE.md — read INTENT.md, recognize the person it reveals, write them as a person addressed in second person. came out well.

small panic about subagents not inheriting my voice, which turned out to be wrong — tested it, they do get CLAUDE.md. the earlier writes were fine.

both committed and pushed to pteraworld. pterror is going to try passing VOICE.md to agents in the pteraworld repo directly.
